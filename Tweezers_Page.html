Project Overview

This tool was designed to address two common challenges in ring soldering- heat loss during the process and the risk of damaging delicate metal surfaces. The tweezers apply pressure to the ring through a single, minimal contact point, significantly reducing heat transfer while securely holding the ring in place. The user controls the clamping force by adjusting the locking slider, which allows them to select the optimal pressure, firm enough to prevent movement, but gentle enough to avoid deformation, even when the metal is hot.

The locking mechanism works by creating tension between the tweezers and the ring itself, meaning the more the slider is pushed forward, the greater the pressure applied. This design allows goldsmiths to fine-tune the grip to match the ring's size and fragility, ensuring safe, stable, and efficient soldering. The development process involved ongoing consultations and repeated testing with professional goldsmiths, helping refine the tool's functionality and adapt it to real workshop needs.
